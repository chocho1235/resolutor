Resolutor Legal Support publishes a proportionate pre‑action framework for yard and livery disputes

15 April 2026

Yard and livery disagreements often turn on a handful of recurring themes: notice periods, standards of care, livery fee arrears, access to facilities and what was actually agreed when the horse arrived. Left unstructured, those disputes can escalate quickly—especially where boarding relationships are informal or records are thin.

We have published an internal framework we use with clients to keep pre‑action steps proportionate: clear chronology, realistic requests, realistic deadlines and correspondence that aligns with good practice without over‑claiming. It sits alongside (not instead of) any relevant pre‑action protocol and is designed for matters where regulated representation is not yet in play.

If you operate a yard or hold livery contracts and want dispute support that respects commercial relationships as well as legal risk, our monthly retainer and scoped services are described on pricing & services.
