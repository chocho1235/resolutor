When a letter before action lands: first steps for operators (equestrian example)

28 March 2026

A letter before action is often the first formal sign that someone intends to escalate. For yards, coaches and retailers, the instinct can be to reply quickly or to go quiet. Neither is ideal without a short internal triage: what is alleged, what does the paperwork show, and what pre‑action practice expects in this type of claim?

We usually recommend assembling a dated bundle early: contracts, notices, invoices, key emails or messages, yard rules, vet instructions and any incident notes. That does not mean sharing everything with the other side immediately; it means you can take advice and respond from a position of clarity rather than memory.
